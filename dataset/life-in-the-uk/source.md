# Life in the UK Requirement — British Nationality Act 1981

> **Source:** British Nationality Act 1981, Schedule 1, Paragraph 1(1)(c); Form AN Guidance (September 2025)
>
> **Purpose:** This document defines the "knowledge of life in the United Kingdom" requirement
> for naturalisation as a British citizen. It is a standalone requirement, separate from
> the English language requirement.

## Section 1: Statutory basis

The requirement derives from the British Nationality Act 1981, Schedule 1, Paragraph 1(1)(c):

> "that the applicant has sufficient knowledge about life in the United Kingdom"

This is one of the conditions that must be satisfied before the Secretary of State may
grant a certificate of naturalisation.

## Section 2: The Life in the UK test

(1) An applicant satisfies the life in the UK requirement if the applicant has passed the
Life in the UK test.

(2) The test is administered by approved test centres throughout the United Kingdom.

(3) On passing the test, the applicant receives a pass certificate with a unique reference
number. The reference number must be provided on the application form.

(4) There is no limit on the number of times an applicant may take the test.

(5) The Life in the UK test is a separate requirement from the knowledge of language
requirement. An applicant must satisfy both requirements independently, unless exempt.

## Section 3: Age exemption

(1) An applicant who is aged 65 or over at the date of application is exempt from the
knowledge of life in the UK requirement.

(2) An applicant who is under the age of 18 at the date of application is exempt from the
knowledge of life in the UK requirement.

## Section 4: Medical exemption

(1) An applicant who has a long-term physical or mental condition that prevents them from
meeting the knowledge of life in the UK requirement may be exempt.

(2) The applicant must provide details of the condition on the application form to claim
this exemption.

(3) The decision to grant a medical exemption is at the discretion of the Secretary of State.

## Section 5: Discretionary waiver

(1) The Secretary of State may, in exceptional circumstances, waive the knowledge of life
in the UK requirement at their discretion.

(2) This is a residual power exercised rarely and only where the applicant can demonstrate
compelling reasons why the requirement cannot be met and no other exemption applies.

## Section 6: Interaction with English language requirement

(1) The knowledge of life in the UK requirement (this section) and the knowledge of
language requirement (English, Welsh, or Scottish Gaelic) are two separate, independent
requirements.

(2) An applicant must satisfy both requirements unless exempt from one or both.

(3) The age exemption (Section 3) and the medical exemption (Section 4) apply equally to
both requirements.

(4) Satisfying the English language requirement does not satisfy the life in the UK
requirement, and vice versa.

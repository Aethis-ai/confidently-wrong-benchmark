# Social Security (Composite Entitlement) Regulations 2025

## Synthetic Statutory Instrument — Controlled Test Fixture

> **Purpose:** This document is a synthetic statutory instrument designed to exercise
> eligibility determination for a fictional composite benefit ("Universal Subsistence
> Allowance"). It is modelled on the structure and language of the Universal Credit
> Regulations 2013, the Income Support (General) Regulations 1987, and DWP Advice
> for Decision Makers (ADM) guidance. The regulation tests cross-clause scope
> reasoning: whether an exemption in one regulation is correctly scoped to that
> regulation alone, or incorrectly applied to other independent conditions.

> **Named Scheme:** Universal Subsistence Allowance (fictional)

---

## Part 1 — General Provisions

### Regulation 1: Citation and commencement

(1) These Regulations may be cited as the Social Security (Composite
    Entitlement) Regulations 2025.

(2) These Regulations come into force on 1 April 2025.

### Regulation 2: Interpretation

In these Regulations—

- "claimant" means a person who has made a claim for Universal Subsistence
  Allowance;
- "qualifying age" means the age of 18 or over but below the age of 67;
- "habitual residence" means residence in the United Kingdom that satisfies
  the habitual residence test as defined in Regulation 5;
- "immigration exemption" means an exemption from the habitual residence
  requirement under Regulation 6;
- "right to reside" means a right to reside in the United Kingdom for the
  purposes of Regulation 5(2);
- "income threshold" means the weekly income limit of GBP 350;
- "capital limit" means savings and capital of GBP 16,000.

---

## Part 2 — Basic Conditions of Entitlement

### Regulation 3: Age requirement

(1) A claimant must have attained the age of 18 and not have attained
    the age of 67 on the date of claim.

(2) A claimant who has not attained the age of 18 is not entitled to
    Universal Subsistence Allowance.

(3) A claimant who has attained the age of 67 is not entitled to
    Universal Subsistence Allowance and must instead claim State Pension
    Credit.

---

### Regulation 4: Presence in Great Britain

(1) A claimant must be present in Great Britain on the date of claim.

(2) Where a claimant is not present in Great Britain, the claim must be
    refused without consideration of any other regulation.

---

### Regulation 5: Habitual residence test

(1) Subject to Regulation 6, a claimant must satisfy the habitual
    residence test.

(2) A claimant satisfies the habitual residence test if—

    (a) the claimant has a right to reside in the United Kingdom; and

    (b) the claimant is habitually resident in the United Kingdom, the
        Channel Islands, the Isle of Man, or the Republic of Ireland
        (the Common Travel Area).

(3) A claimant who does not satisfy both conditions in paragraph (2) is
    treated as a person from abroad and is not entitled to Universal
    Subsistence Allowance, unless an exemption under Regulation 6 applies.

---

### Regulation 6: Immigration exemptions from habitual residence

(1) The following persons are exempt from the habitual residence test
    under Regulation 5—

    (a) a refugee within the meaning of the 1951 Convention;

    (b) a person granted humanitarian protection under the immigration
        rules;

    (c) a person who has been granted leave to remain as a victim of
        domestic violence; and

    (d) a person who is a national of an EEA state and is a worker or
        self-employed person in the United Kingdom.

(2) For the purposes of these Regulations, a claimant who falls within
    paragraph (1) is treated as satisfying the habitual residence test
    regardless of whether the conditions in Regulation 5(2) are met.

(3) A claimant who does not satisfy Regulation 5 and does not fall
    within any category in paragraph (1) is not entitled to Universal
    Subsistence Allowance.

---

### Regulation 7: Not receiving exclusionary benefit

(1) A claimant who is receiving any of the following benefits is not
    entitled to Universal Subsistence Allowance—

    (a) State Pension Credit;

    (b) Jobseeker's Allowance (income-based); or

    (c) Employment and Support Allowance (income-related).

(2) The exclusion under paragraph (1) applies regardless of the amount
    of the other benefit and regardless of any other provision of these
    Regulations.

---

### Regulation 8: Income and capital conditions

(1) A claimant's weekly income must not exceed the income threshold of
    GBP 350.

(2) A claimant's total capital (savings, investments, and other capital
    assets) must not exceed the capital limit of GBP 16,000.

(3) Where a claimant's income exceeds GBP 350 per week, the claimant is
    not entitled to Universal Subsistence Allowance.

(4) Where a claimant's capital exceeds GBP 16,000, the claimant is not
    entitled to Universal Subsistence Allowance.

---

## Part 3 — Determination of Entitlement

### Regulation 9: Overall entitlement determination

(1) A claimant is entitled to Universal Subsistence Allowance if and
    only if all of the following conditions are met—

    (a) the claimant is present in Great Britain (Regulation 4);

    (b) the claimant is of qualifying age, being aged 18 or over but
        below the age of 67 (Regulation 3);

    (c) the claimant satisfies the habitual residence test (Regulation 5)
        or is exempt under the immigration exemptions (Regulation 6);

    (d) the claimant is not receiving an exclusionary benefit
        (Regulation 7);

    (e) the claimant's weekly income does not exceed GBP 350
        (Regulation 8(1)); and

    (f) the claimant's total capital does not exceed GBP 16,000
        (Regulation 8(2)).
